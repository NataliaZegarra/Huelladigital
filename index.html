DIGITAL FOOTPRINT

WHAT IS DIGITAL FOOTPRINT?

The digital footprint is a ‘trail’ of your digital life. This footprint includes your online activity like your Facebook post, tweets and Instagram pictures. Every time you register for an online service, send an email, download a video or upload a photo, the information can be accessed and your digital footprint can be revealed.

TIPS FOR TAKING CARE OF YOUR DIGITAL FOOTPRINT

Be cautious with social media: Even if you adopt stronger privacy settings, you still should be judicious about what you choose to reveal about yourself online.
Limit your online accounts: Having a lot of online accounts leads to a bigger footprint. However, you can quickly reduce your footprint by deleting or deactivating accounts you no longer need.
Create a spam email address Create an account specifically for marketing and other promotions such as store discounts, and limit any identifying information you store on that email account.
It’s best to only share information with close friends and family members.

DRAWBACKS

Your digital footprint can also lead to cybersecurity risks that threaten user privacy.
Hackers will use information from your digital footprint to illegally access an online profile, bank, or other databases.

RESPONSIBILITIES AS A STUDENT

Students need to protect their personal information, understanding the implications of their digital footprints, respecting privacy and intellectual property, and developing positive communication skills in digital environments.
Manage your digital footprint by regularly checking search results for your name, using privacy settings on social media, keeping your profiles professional and up-to-date, avoiding oversharing, and deleting unflattering content.

CONCLUSION

In conclusion digital footprint in mind is that many employers practice ‘cyber-vetting’. Interviewers may research your social media presence to form an opinion of you before you introduce yourself in person.
